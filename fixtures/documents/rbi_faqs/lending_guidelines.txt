# RBI Guidelines on Fair Lending Practices

## Consumer Rights
Every borrower has the right to:
- Receive a loan sanction letter with all terms clearly mentioned
- Know the total cost of borrowing including processing fees and charges
- Receive a copy of loan agreement before disbursement
- Prepay the loan without penalty (as per RBI guidelines)
- Receive a No Objection Certificate (NOC) upon loan closure

## Interest Rate Disclosure
Banks must disclose:
- Annual Percentage Rate (APR) including all charges
- Basis of interest calculation (reducing balance/flat rate)
- Floating rate reset frequency and methodology
- Conditions under which rates can be revised

## Grievance Redressal
If you have a complaint:
1. Contact bank's customer care within 30 days
2. If unresolved, escalate to bank's nodal officer
3. If still unresolved, approach Banking Ombudsman
4. Complaints can be filed online at RBI's CMS portal

## Fair Practices Code
Banks must:
- Not discriminate based on caste, religion, or gender
- Provide loan documents in vernacular language
- Display interest rates and charges prominently
- Follow RBI-mandated recovery practices
- Not harass or use unfair recovery methods

## Third-Party Collection Agencies
- Banks hiring collection agents must ensure they follow RBI guidelines
- Agents cannot visit borrower's residence between 7 PM and 7 AM
- Harassment or threatening language is strictly prohibited
- Borrowers can report violations to RBI

For detailed guidelines, visit: www.rbi.org.in
